# E-Commerce-Customer-Intelligence-Dashboard
## Project Title / Headline
E-Commerce Customer Intelligence: RFM Segmentation & Revenue Contribution Analysis
## Short Description / Purpose
This project analyzes an e-commerce transaction dataset to understand customer purchasing behavior and revenue contribution patterns. Using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) segmentation, the analysis identifies high-value customer groups and evaluates their impact on overall revenue. The resulting dashboard provides insights that can support targeted marketing, customer retention strategies, and revenue optimization.
## Tech Stack
Python (Pandas, NumPy) – Data processing and feature engineering
Python (Matplotlib) – Exploratory visualizations
Power BI – Dashboard development and data visualization
DAX – KPI calculations and interactive metrics
Jupyter Notebook – Data analysis workflow
## Data Source
Dataset: E-Commerce Online Retail Dataset from kaggle.
The dataset contains transaction-level information for an online retail business.
Dataset size:
350,000+ transaction records
4,000+ unique customers
Key attributes include:
Invoice Number
Customer ID
Product Description
Quantity Purchased
Unit Price
Transaction Date
Revenue
The dataset allows analysis of customer purchasing patterns and revenue distribution.
# Features / Highlights
## Business Problem
E-commerce businesses often struggle to identify which customers contribute the most to revenue and which segments require retention focus.
Without proper customer segmentation, businesses may:
Allocate marketing resources inefficiently
Miss opportunities to retain high-value customers
Fail to identify customers with declining engagement
Customer intelligence analysis helps businesses focus on high-value and high-potential customers.
Goal of the Dashboard
The goal of the dashboard is to provide insights into customer purchasing behavior and revenue contribution, enabling stakeholders to:
Identify high-value customer segments
Understand purchasing frequency and recency patterns
Monitor revenue contribution by customer group
Detect opportunities for targeted marketing campaigns
# Walkthrough of Key Visuals
## 1️. KPI Overview Section
Displays key e-commerce metrics such as:
Total Revenue
Total Customers
Total Transactions
Average Order Value (AOV)
These metrics provide a quick overview of overall business performance.
## 2️. Revenue Contribution by Customer Segment
This visual shows how different customer segments contribute to overall revenue.
It highlights high-value customer groups that drive a significant portion of revenue.
## 3️. RFM Customer Segmentation
Customers are grouped into segments based on:
Recency (how recently a customer made a purchase)
Frequency (how often they purchase)
Monetary Value (how much they spend)
This segmentation helps identify:
High-value customers
Loyal customers
At-risk customers
## 4️. Purchase Frequency Analysis
This visual analyzes how often customers place orders, helping identify repeat buyers versus occasional buyers.
## 5️. Revenue Distribution Across Customers
This visual highlights how revenue is distributed across the customer base, revealing whether revenue is concentrated among a small group of customers.

## Business Insights
The analysis revealed several key insights:
A small segment of high-value customers contributes a large share of overall revenue.
Customers who purchase frequently tend to generate significantly higher lifetime value.
Some customer segments show low recency but high historical value, indicating potential churn risk.
Understanding customer segmentation helps businesses design targeted marketing and retention strategies.
## Business Impact
These insights help businesses:
Focus retention strategies on high-value customers
Design personalized marketing campaigns
Improve customer lifetime value (CLV)
Allocate marketing resources more efficiently
Optimize revenue growth strategies
